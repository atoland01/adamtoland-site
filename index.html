Education

My academic path looks unconventional on paper — a TEFL qualification in Barcelona, a double degree in mathematics and Spanish, then a Master's in technology entrepreneurship. But there's a single thread running through all of it: problem solving. Mathematics taught me to reason from first principles. Language showed me that the same rigour applies to human systems — grammar, culture, meaning. Entrepreneurship turned out to be both of those things at once, but with real stakes. Each degree was the next version of the same obsession.
